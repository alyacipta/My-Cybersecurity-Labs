### 🧐 What I Learned Today:
Digital Forensics is the application of computer science to investigate digital evidence for legal purposes. I explored the fundamental difference between two types of investigations:
1. **Public-Sector:** Investigations by government/law enforcement (Criminal/Civil).
2. **Private-Sector:** Investigations by corporations (Policy violations/Internal threats).

### 🔍 Key Insight: Identifying Digital Evidence
In a mock crime scene scenario involving a desk with multiple devices (Smartphone, Camera, SD cards), I identified the **Laptop** as the primary item of interest.

**Why the Laptop?**
- **Central Hub:** It acts as the "Command Center" where data from peripheral devices (SD cards, cameras) is processed, stored, and communicated.
- **Evidence Depth:** Unlike smaller media, a laptop contains complex artifacts like **System Registries**, **Application Logs**, and **Browser History** which are vital for establishing a timeline [linimasa] of events.
